About Me

Hello, I'm Sandipan Samanta - a 4th year Integrated MSc student at NISER (National Institute of Science Education and Research) Bhubaneswar with a deep passion for mathematics, coding, and innovation. My journey in math began in childhood and really took off when I started tackling challenging problems in math olympiads. Those early experiences ignited a lifelong love for the subject.


Academically, my focus lies in complex and harmonic analysis. I've explored topics like Fourier analysis, Hardy-Littlewood maximal operators, and gained a solid understanding of Lp spaces—even if I'm still learning the finer points. Currently, I'm delving into Bourgain-Morrey spaces, always eager to push my boundaries further.


Over the course of my studies, I've been fortunate to receive academic excellence awards in semesters 3 and 5, and I'm honored to have been among the batch toppers. My hands-on experiences include two summer internship projects at NISER and a machine learning project on the Voronoi area game (more details on my Academics page). I've also had the chance to lead the MathematiX club (2023-24) and serve as a core member of the RoboTech Club for almost a year.


Outside the classroom, I'm passionate about chess, robotics, and coding (Python is my language of choice). Recently, I've even dipped my toes into astrophotography—embracing a new challenge while staying true to my analytical roots.


I wouldn't be where I am today without the unwavering support of my seniors, especially Archisman Bhattacharya and Ayanava Mandal, as well as my amazing batchmates. Their mentorship and camaraderie have been invaluable throughout my journey.


Welcome to my world—where math meets creativity, and learning never stops.
